Amala

Description

Amala is a traditional Nigerian meal eaten by the Yoruba ethnic group in the country’s western states. It’s manufactured using unripe plantain flour, yam flour, and/or cassava flour. Liver, and a little bit of oil for crisping it up.

As a popular Nigerian dish, it’s difficult to go to any of the local bukkas in Lagos or Ibadan without seeing this dish on the menu. The beautiful thing about this Yoruba special is that it is quick and easy to make, but if you aren’t careful, you could end up with lumps that make it unappealing to eat. If you struggle with making lump free, fluffy, smooth Amala, relax because with this recipe you’ll get it under control.

Ingredients

  • Flour (cassava or yam flour)
  • Water

Steps

  1. Pour the quantity of water you want to use in a pot and let it heat to a boiling point.
  2. Once the water is boiled, turn out the fire and gradually begin to add your flour.
  3. Ensure you do not add the flour to the water when still on the fire, that way it does not form lumps.
  4. Turn the flour with a wooden stick for a while, add little water and put it back on the fire to cook on low heat for about 5mins so as to make it cook properly.
  5. You can use one of your clean finger to feel it while still on fire to be sure it is neither too soft nor to hard add some hot water.
  6. Stir well until it very smooth.
  7. Wrap with nylon and put it in a cooler , so as to keep it warm
